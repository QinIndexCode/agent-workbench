# SCC-Batch v2.0 Launch FAQ

## Audience FAQ

**Q: What is SCC-Batch v2.0?**
A: SCC-Batch v2.0 is the next-generation task orchestration platform for enterprise workflow automation. It introduces API v2 endpoints, a redesigned operator dashboard, and improved CLI tooling to help teams manage complex backend workflows with greater reliability and visibility.

**Q: When is SCC-Batch v2.0 available?**
A: Public general availability is June 23. A limited beta for design partners runs June 9–13. You can sign up for early access on our landing page.

**Q: Is v2.0 backward compatible with v1?**
A: The core concepts are the same, but API v2 introduces some breaking changes. A migration guide will be published two weeks before launch. Legacy v1 migration tooling is planned as a separate workstream.

**Q: What does SCC-Batch v2.0 cost?**
A: Pricing details will be announced alongside the public launch on June 23. Existing v1 customers will receive migration pricing and a transition period.

**Q: How do I get support during and after launch?**
A: Our customer success team, led by Aisha Patel, will offer office-hours sessions during beta and launch week. Standard support channels remain available, and a dedicated launch support queue will be active for the first 72 hours.

**Q: Will there be a mobile app?**
A: A mobile app is planned for v2.1, which follows the v2.0 release. It is out of scope for the current launch.

## Internal FAQ

**Q: What are the success criteria for this launch?**
A: Three primary metrics — 500 sign-ups within 30 days, zero P0 incidents in the first 72 hours, and an NPS score of 40+ from the beta cohort.

**Q: Who owns the launch?**
A: Engineering release is owned by Priya Nair (backup: Marcus Chen). Marketing and comms are owned by Elena Vasquez (backup: Tyler Brooks). QA is owned by Jordan Ellis (backup: Sam Okafor). Customer success is owned by Aisha Patel (backup: Leo Nguyen). DevRel and docs are owned by Chris Romero (backup: Dana Kim).

**Q: What is the biggest risk?**
A: API v2 breaking changes causing partner integration failures is the highest-impact risk. Mitigation includes publishing the migration guide early and offering office-hours support. Infrastructure capacity is the second-highest risk and will be load-tested at 3x projected peak.

**Q: What happens if beta feedback reveals critical UX gaps?**
A: The timeline includes a 3-day buffer between beta close and GA launch specifically to address priority fixes identified during the beta window.

**Q: What is the post-launch plan?**
A: A metrics review and incident retrospective are scheduled for June 30, along with the kickoff for v2.1 planning. The mobile app and marketplace integrations are candidates for v2.1.

**Q: How do we handle press if coverage is delayed?**
A: Embargoed briefings will be seeded to five key outlets. If press coverage is absent, the team will pivot to owned-channel distribution — blog post, email sequences, and social campaign — all prepared as part of the marketing prep phase (Jun 9–20).
